A once-in-a-lifetime overseas voluntary experience motivates the thirty-year-old

Reported by Melissa Ko

Inspired by VolTra’s slogan: “we are not only our city’s citizens but also the citizens of the world”, Emily Wong Ka-yi took a step forward to open her eyes by joining working holiday scheme at her age of 30.


The charitable organization that inspired Wong deeply is called VolTra, dedicated to promoting international voluntary service in Hong Kong in the hope of promoting world peace and global citizenship since 2009.


“Better late than never,” said Wong. “Thirty is the age I started to realise gaining international exposure means more to me than just earning money. Thirty is a good start.”


Her age number as a reminder →

Wong joined her first-ever project of voluntary work planned by VolTra to Turkey in 2015. During the two-week overseas voluntary experience, she collaborated with nine other volunteers from all over the world to help the local schools to pick olives from the trees.


The two-week VolTra voluntary experience had influenced her decision to take part in the two-year working holiday scheme one year later, said Wong who first worked as an usherette at the cinema in Edinburg, and afterwards worked for three different hostels in exchange for accommodation.


“Working abroad is a challenging but unforgettable experience.” “It wasn’t easy at the beginning but it’s definitely a great life lesson which taught me humility and the way to let go of anxiety.”


With a growing interest in travel, Wong regularly puts a certain amount of money into her travel saving account and travel for a few weeks every year after volunteering in Turkey. But travelling itself could not satisfy her longing for “completeness and wholeness.”


Brew with heart: a chance for the coffee exchange →

Wong said, “I didn’t feel complete at all until I live outside of Hong Kong for a longer period of time and make international friends.” She indicated that working holiday programme had made her born again.


Wong said candidly that her English might not be as good as other working holidayers from Hong Kong but she thought English was not the major part that drove her to start the life in Edinburgh.


“Making new friends from all around the world is my ultimate goal throughout my journey and I’ve made it,” Wong said unregretfully.
