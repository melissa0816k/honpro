General information

What is Working Holiday Scheme?

The working holiday programmes is based on bilateral arrangements, making it possible for the youth of Hong Kong and its partner countries to enter each country primarily for the purpose of spending holidays while allowing young people to engage in employment as an incidental activity of their holidays to subsidise their travel. The scheme also provides the youth with wider opportunities to appreciate the culture in the partner countries.

Working holiday visa?

If you’re 30 or below and struggle to a company to hire you before you move, there is always the option of a working holiday visa, which allows you to live and work in one of the partner countries, including Australia, Sweden, United Kingdom, France, Germany, New Zealand, Hungary, Austria, Japan, South Korea, Ireland and Canada.

Are you eligible to join?

  • Be aged between 18 and 30 at the time of making application
  • Be ordinarily resident in the HKSAR and hold a valid HKSAR or British National (Overseas) passport
  • Hold a return travel ticket or provide a proof of financial resources to purchase such ticket and support your expenses in the host economy
  • Take out insurance plans throughout your stay in the host country, except Australia
