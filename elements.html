FAQs

Three FAQs about working holiday you need to know

Planning a working holiday in a country with incredible nature, culture and people but you’ve come across some questions on it? Below are answers to the frequently asked questions for the Working Holiday Scheme. For more information, you may check out General Information.

1. Is there any restriction on the number of times of participation under the Working Holiday Scheme?

Participants, in general, can visit the same economy only once under the scheme but they are allowed to apply for a working holiday visa to other partner countries, as a Hong Kong citizen.

2. Will the HKSAR government provide subsidies for the participants?

Unlike university exchange programmes, participants cannot get an allowance from the government because they are expected to be financially independent if they want to challenge themselves by joining the scheme. They should work out their own itineraries, prepare enough living and travelling expenses without any financial support from either schools or the government.

3. What are popular jobs with a working holiday visa? (sharing from former working holiday makers from Hong Kong)

Retail & sales

Participants may comparatively find it easy to get a retail sales job no matter they’re experienced or not because there is always a high rate of turnover. Working holiday makers, therefore, tend to first work as a sales representative in a souvenir shop to earn a living in short run.

Food & drink industry

Working as a waiter or barista is also the top form of employment for working holidaymakers. Most Hongkongers consider working in Chinese restaurants. Some of them even attended some coffee training courses before joining the scheme so that they can find a job at cafes with their skills.

Hospitality

This industry is one of the popular choices among young working holiday makers from Hong Kong because they not only have a chance to work with locals and meet travellers from all over the world but also enjoy free accommodation.

Tour guide

As the number of Chinese tourists to Europe continues growing, more Chinese travel agencies are established to meet the needs by recruiting Hongkongers who are regarded as bilingual people who can speak both Mandarin and English well.
