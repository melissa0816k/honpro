Waking up to a bell sound and chase the teenage dream

Reported by Melissa Ko

Kiwi Ngan came back from a one-year working holiday, the time she had spent not only on some faraway castles but also striving for independence and work-life balance.


Like many other white-collar workers in Hong Kong, Ngan was busy with handling a pile of projects in the office where the fast pace of life sometimes drove her nuts. But one day she was stopped by an almighty, week-long ding-dong sound from the secondary school nearby. She started to realise she “had no much time left.”


At the age of 25, Ngan left her comfort zone to chase her teenage dream for the first time because she thought she neglected some beautiful moments of life in the past few years.


Her little dream was to work in the fields of nature and English-speaking environment, but the chance to do so never came along until two years ago she was given an application form of working holiday scheme by her college friend.


Ngan said, “I’m still so thankful for the sound of the bell and my friend. They’re kind of virtual push. This made my dream finally come true.”


“I know I only live once so I’d put into practice,” she added. “it meant a lot and was really surprising to hear I was granted the visa to the UK because I think this is the best place where I can learn English and enjoy life while working outside of Hong Kong.”


She worked at a touristy shop selling cashmere scarves in Edinburgh while pursuing another goal close to her heart - learning authentic English and making international friends to widen her horizons.


“I’m easy. I mean I don’t care which job it is as long as I can learn from it. I came for working holiday just for learning,” Ngan said.


About 10 years ago, Ngan knew the working holiday scheme in Australia through the leaflets distributed by her secondary school. Since then, Ngan planted the seed of the dream into her heart as a working holidayer.
