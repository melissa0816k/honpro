Her age number as a reminder

Reported by Melissa Ko

Tired of being stuck in a rut with no chance of promotion, doing the same thing day after day? Dream of packing it all in to hit the road and explore the far corners of the world?


About one year and nine months ago, Janis Wong Yi-ching did just that, quitting her hospitality job in Island Shangri-La to set off on a journey of growth in England without a specific end date but within her visa’s validity. She revealed how the reality of almost-two-year working holiday trip - while amazing - could be so challenging.


When, in 2011, Janis had her first job in a hotel working as a receptionist after graduation, working extra hours every single working day seemed to become part of her daily routine. “I had been working in a hotel for more than five years but I couldn’t see any promotion opportunities,” said Wong.


“I want some changes in my life before the age of 30,” she continued with an affirmative smile. “I’m almost 30 and this might be my last chance to try something different and impulsive.”


The idea of going overseas to work came after she decided her career would not fit in with the life she wanted to have. Wong left her Hong Kong-based job and managed to find a full-time job in her friend’s gastropub located in Newcastle.


She analysed the data, handled with paperwork and did some setups there for nearly nine months and, afterwards, she moved to Edinburgh, the capital of Scotland where job opportunities were rumoured to be better.


Without any friends there, she plucked up the courage to knock on doors armed with a stack of resumes. “The more-than-a-month unemployment period was one of the most emotionally draining times in my life because I was running out of money at that time,” she said. “I was looking for something related to hospitality and tourism rather than catering service.”


Even so, she did not lose her passion for the industry. Continuously sending resumes to let her find the current job - a front desk position in the hotel.


Despite her UK working visa being expired soon, the avid 29-year-old explorer has joined another newly launched working holiday scheme to Sweden and is set to begin her trip in the coming July.
