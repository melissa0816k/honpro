God pushes her into stepping outside of the comfort zone

Reported by Melissa Ko

While many new grads still struggled to find the right fit for their skills and interest, Miranda Ma Yuen-ting has already figured out what to do before graduation - go somewhere far away from home and experience the grace of God.


Travelling solo as a young woman always comes with its own particular set of concerns while most of the female solo travellers have been described as adventurous, bold and intrepid. Ma, 22, a graduate of Hong Kong Baptist University, stepped outside of the box and embarked her own journey last October.


With a well-maintained and carefully-cut short hairstyle, Ma has been ready to embrace the challenges in a strange country that she has never been to in order to “encounter the true gospel of grace.” “I don’t want to just experience his[God’s] love in Hong Kong,” she said.


“Religion is the main factor that drove me to join the scheme,” Miranda said. “I want to experience his grace and existence while being outside of my familiar place, Hong Kong.”


She described herself as being highly anxious, worrisome while being involved in unfamiliar situations and activities. But the help of church buddies from Hong Kong and Scotland made her “slightly calm down.” She believed it was the work of God.


“No matter how well you plan, changes to your plan are always inevitable,” she said.


“I do my part and the God will do the rest,” she said. Ma gathered the courage to find the decent shared house and a sales job to earn a living after a month of arrival.


Speaking of whether to stay until the working visa expires, Ma said: “I don’t want to stay longer because the place I’m here now doesn’t belong to me.”
