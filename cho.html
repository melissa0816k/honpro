“Lucky” girl got her first management job in Hong Kong after working holiday

Reported by Melissa Ko

Dorothy Cho Ka-yan, three years ago, decided to leave the city for a year to refresh her minds and gain some international exposures at her age of 30. She now is a public relation manager.


“If I had to use only one world to describe my working holiday journey, it would be lucky. The experience I gained from the trip made me have the advantage of finding a more suitable job when I returned to Hong Kong,” Cho said.


Tired of the stressful working environment in Hong Kong, Cho and her boyfriend went to different countries to start their working holiday. Cho went to the UK while her boyfriend travelled to Germany. She explained, “we both believed that it could make us grow up more and have more topics to share with each other.”


Cho worked in the award-winning luxury hotel, Lindeth Fell Country House located in Bowness-on-Windermere during the working holiday period.

With her journalistic background, she was not only required to serve the customers but also to do some translation tasks such as writing some descriptions to introduce the hotel facilities and the tourist spots nearby in simplified Chinese and making brochures.


She said, “I was so surprised that my supervisors gave me this, such an important job.”


Home and away: getting a work permit and stay on →

She added, “I was actually benefited from this unique working experience after getting back to Hong Kong and looking for the full-time job.”


No regrets abroad: “This is my best decision ever!” →

“I quickly got my public relation management job at NGO (Non-governmental organisation) because my employer thought that I not only had relevant experience in communication but also had overseas editing experience,” she continued.


Cho, who had worked as an editor and receptionist in the hotel for three months to help the hotel deal with the peak season, described herself as “the luckiest working holidayer in the world” because she was the first and the only working holidayer the hotel has hired so far.


She said the hotel, with a spacious garden and elegant decorations, has eft Cho with an extraordinary impression.
